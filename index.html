âš–ï¸ Exercise 8.3: Diet Effectiveness Comparison

Statistical comparison of two diet programs using relative frequency histograms to analyze weight loss patterns. This exercise explores measures of central tendency (mean), dispersion (standard deviation), and distribution shapes to evaluate intervention effectiveness and consistency across participants. The analysis demonstrates Diet A's superior performance with higher average weight loss (5.3 kg vs 3.7 kg) and more consistent outcomes (SD 2.5 kg vs 2.8 kg).

Key Findings:

  • Diet A: Superior results - Mean 5.3 kg loss, consistent outcomes (SD = 2.5 kg), symmetric bell-shaped distribution
  • Diet B: Lower effectiveness - Mean 3.7 kg loss, higher variability (SD = 2.8 kg), more spread with slight right skew
  • Reliability: Diet A shows predictable outcomes with nearly all participants losing weight (minimum = -1.7 kg)
  • Diet B Variability: Some participants gained weight (minimum = -4.1 kg), peak frequency at 2 kg indicates modest results for many
  • Recommendation: Diet A preferred due to higher average results and consistent, predictable outcomes
