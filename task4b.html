Task 4B: Linear Regression

This task applies linear regression to model the relationship between the average population and GDP per capita of countries from 2001 to 2020. We used Scikit-learn to compute the regression line and evaluate the fit.

Notebook Access

  • Download/View Notebook (.ipynb)

Results

  • The linear regression model resulted in a coefficient (slope) of approximately 0.000017 and an intercept around 10,514, indicating that for each additional person in a country's population, the GDP per capita increases slightly—although the effect is minimal.
  • The model's R² score was ~0.38, suggesting that the linear model explains around 38% of the variance in GDP per capita based on population. This indicates that the model has limited explanatory power and is likely underfitting the data.
  • The scatter plot with the regression line shows a wide dispersion of data points around the regression line, reinforcing that the linear relationship is weak.
  • This result emphasizes that while population is a relevant feature, it should not be used in isolation to predict GDP per capita. Incorporating additional variables—such as education level, resource availability, and government expenditure—would enhance the model.
